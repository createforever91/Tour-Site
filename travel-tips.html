Before you explore Cartagena, avoid common tourist mistakes and travel with confidence using these local tips.

Watch Out

Avoid These Common Mistakes

Simple mistakes can cost you money or ruin your experience — here's what to watch for

warning Safety

Avoid Flashy Jewelry

Keep high-value items in your hotel safe. Avoid wearing expensive watches or visible gold chains while walking.

block Scams

Don't Accept Free Gifts

Politely decline "free" samples of massage or oysters on the beach; they always come with a high price tag after.

opacity Health

Avoid Drinking Tap Water

Always opt for bottled or filtered water. The local tap water can cause stomach upset for visitors.

visibility_off Awareness

Don't Leave Drinks Unattended

Never leave your drink unattended in bars or clubs. Keep your glass in sight at all times.

payments Finance

Always Ask the Price First

Avoid "market surprises." Agree on a total price before accepting any food, drink, or taxi ride.

trending_down Budget

Avoid Overpaying

Research standard rates. Taxis in the city shouldn't exceed 15,000 COP for most short distances.

Pro Tips

Travel Like a Local

These simple tips will make your trip smoother, safer, and more enjoyable

directions_car

Private Transport

Use Uber or pre-booked private cars for predictable pricing and safety.

currency_exchange

Exchange Safely

Use official exchange houses or ATMs inside banks rather than street changers.

water_drop

Stay Hydrated

The Caribbean humidity is intense. Carry a reusable bottle with filtered water.

lock

Keep Belongings Secure

Use a cross-body bag and keep it in front of you in crowded markets.

translate

Learn Basic Spanish

Knowing "¿Cuánto cuesta?" and numbers goes a long way with vendors.

handshake

Negotiate Respectfully

Respectful negotiation is expected in markets, but don't lowball artisans.

restaurant

Try Authentic Local Food

Arepa de Huevo and coconut rice are must-try Caribbean staples.

shield

Stay in Safe Areas

Stick to well-known areas like Centro, Getsemaní, and Bocagrande, especially at night.
